Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 20, 2026

HST Log is a Health and Safety Team reporting app used by churches and authorized team members to create, manage, and share incident reports and daily logs.

This Privacy Policy explains what information HST Log collects, stores, and uses.

Information We Collect

HST Log may collect information you provide directly in the app, including:

  • Account information, such as email address and password credentials handled through Supabase authentication.
  • Church membership information, such as church name, display name, role, membership status, and team access permissions.
  • Team invitation information, such as invited user email addresses, display names, roles, invitation status, and the user who sent the invitation.
  • Incident report information, such as date, time, location, reported by, incident type, severity, people involved, summary, narrative, actions taken, witness notes, EMS involvement, law enforcement involvement, staff notification, follow-up status, and follow-up notes.
  • Daily log information, such as service date, shift, team lead, team members on duty, attendance estimate, doors opened, shift completion, daily summary, patrol notes, safety checks, and handoff notes.
  • Photos or image attachments that users choose to add to reports.
  • App settings, including theme preferences and saved sharing contacts.
  • PDF/share activity initiated by the user when the user chooses to share a report.

Users should avoid entering unnecessary personal or sensitive information and should include only what is needed for legitimate health, safety, operational, or reporting purposes.

How We Use Information

HST Log uses information to:

  • Authenticate users and manage account access.
  • Connect users to their church or team.
  • Enforce role-based permissions for church admins, team leaders, and team members.
  • Create, save, view, edit, and manage incident reports and daily logs.
  • Upload and retrieve report photo attachments.
  • Generate shareable PDF reports.
  • Invite team members and manage pending invitations.
  • Send password reset or invite-related emails.
  • Maintain local app settings and limited local report availability.

HST Log does not use your information for advertising, third-party ad targeting, or sale to data brokers.

Cloud Storage

HST Log stores account, membership, team, report, and attachment data using Supabase cloud services. Reports are associated with the selected church so authorized users for that church can access the appropriate records based on their role and membership.

Photo attachments are uploaded to cloud storage and linked to the related report.

Local Storage

HST Log may also store a local copy or cache of records and settings on the device. This helps the app display saved records and can preserve a local copy if cloud saving temporarily fails.

Local data may remain on the device until deleted in the app, cleared through device storage settings, or removed by uninstalling the app.

Email and Sharing

Automatic email delivery is currently turned off in the active app flow. To email or message a report, users can open Saved Records and choose to share a PDF.

When a user shares a PDF, the selected destination, such as Mail, Messages, or another sharing app, handles the transmission. Once shared outside HST Log, the receiving app, email provider, messaging provider, and recipients may retain copies under their own policies.

The backend includes support for provider-backed email delivery, such as Resend or SMTP, but this should only be considered active if email delivery is enabled in production.

Authentication and Passwords

User accounts are managed through Supabase authentication. Passwords are handled by Supabase authentication systems. HST Log does not display user passwords.

Users may request password reset emails. Invite links may allow invited users to create or set a password for their account.

Who Can Access Information

Report and membership information is intended to be accessible only to authorized users connected to the relevant church.

Depending on role, authorized users may be able to:

  • View reports and logs for their church.
  • Submit new reports.
  • Edit reports.
  • Manage team members.
  • Invite new users.
  • Change roles or remove church access.

Church admins and other authorized leaders are responsible for granting access only to appropriate users.

Third-Party Service Providers

HST Log uses third-party service providers to operate the app, including:

  • Supabase for authentication, database storage, membership data, and file storage.
  • Railway for backend hosting.
  • Email providers, if configured, for invitation, password reset, or report delivery emails.
  • Device sharing services selected by the user when sharing a PDF.

These providers process information as needed to provide app functionality.

Data Retention

Cloud records may be retained for as long as the church or app administrator maintains them, unless deleted according to available app controls or administrative procedures.

Local records may remain on the device until deleted, cleared, or the app is uninstalled.

Shared PDFs, emails, exported files, screenshots, printed copies, or reports sent to other people are not controlled by HST Log after sharing.

Deleting Information

Users may be able to delete local records or request that an authorized church admin remove access or delete records, depending on the app’s current permissions and administrative process.

Removing a user from a church may remove that user’s access to church records, but it may not automatically delete the user’s account or historical records created by that user.

Deleting information from HST Log does not delete copies already shared by email, message, PDF export, screenshot, backup, or other external systems.

Photos and Device Permissions

If a user chooses to attach photos, the app may request access to the camera or photo library. HST Log only uploads photos that the user selects or captures for attachment to a report.

Security

HST Log is intended for sensitive operational and safety records. Users should protect their devices with a passcode or biometric lock and should only share reports with authorized recipients.

Cloud data is protected through the security controls of Supabase, Railway, and the app’s role-based access rules. However, no storage or transmission method can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

Children and Minors

HST Log is not intended for use by children. Reports may include information about minors if entered by authorized users. Users should follow their church or organization’s policies and include only the minimum necessary information.

Organizational Responsibilities

If HST Log is used by a church or organization, that organization is responsible for deciding who may access the app, how reports should be created, how long records should be retained, and how sensitive incident information should be handled.

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated if HST Log’s features, service providers, or data practices change.
